### Outsourcing Talent: Inside the High-Skilled Immigration Market

Abstract: In the U.S., firms hire high-skill immigrants through the H-1B visa program, which allocates a fixed number of visas each year via lottery. This capped, lottery-based system has led firms to increasingly rely on third‑party staffing intermediaries when direct hiring is constrained and uncertain. By merging firm-level lottery outcomes with detailed visa application data, I first show that firms constrained by the lottery reallocate their labor demand toward outsourcing firms. I then develop a structural model in which workers sort based on preferences, firms hire in two stages, and intermediaries allocate labor to maximize revenue. Using the estimated model, I plan to simulate counterfactual visa policies to quantify welfare impacts on foreign‑born and native workers, as well as U.S firms, studying how intermediaries mediate the distribution of policy‑induced gains and losses, and whether alternative regulatory designs can reduce the distortions introduced by the current system.
